# Synesthesia and Sound-to-Colour Mappings

Synesthesia is a neurological phenomenon where one sensory perception (e.g. sound) automatically triggers another (e.g. colour). While not universal, certain consistent associations have been documented across composers and listeners.

## Documented Composer Synesthesia

### Alexander Scriabin (1872–1915)
Scriabin invented a complete colour-key correspondence system used in his *Prometheus: Poem of Fire* (1910), which included a colour organ.

| Note | Colour |
|---|---|
| C | Red |
| G | Orange-pink |
| D | Yellow |
| A | Green |
| E | Pearly blue / sky blue |
| B | Blue, similar to E |
| F♯ | Bright blue |
| D♭ | Violet |
| A♭ | Purple |
| E♭ | Steely metallic grey |
| B♭ | Steel with metallic glitter |
| F | Deep red |

### Olivier Messiaen (1908–1992)
Messiaen explicitly linked specific chord types to specific colours, especially complex polychords:
- "Mode of limited transposition #2" → blue-violet
- "Mode #3" → orange with red and gold
- "Mode #6" → various shades of grey
- "Augmented chord" → reddish-orange

### Nikolai Rimsky-Korsakov

|---|---|
| C major | White |

## Cross-Modal Research (cognitive science)

When non-synesthetes are asked to match colours to sounds, robust patterns emerge:

- **Higher pitch** → lighter, brighter, more saturated colours
- **Lower pitch** → darker, more muted colours
- **Brighter timbre** (more high frequencies) → yellow, white
- **Duller timbre** (more low frequencies) → red, brown
- **Loud** → red, orange, yellow (high arousal)
- **Soft** → blue, purple, green (low arousal)
- **Fast tempo** → bright, vivid hues
- **Slow tempo** → dark, muted hues

## Practical Image-to-Audio Mappings

When SpectraVerse converts an image to audio, the dominant colour cluster maps:

| Dominant colour | Suggested key | Suggested timbre |
|---|---|---|
| Red | F major or D minor | Brassy, warm, full | 
| Orange | C major | Reedy, oboe-like, warm |
| Yellow | D major | Bright trumpet, piccolo |
| Green | F major | Flute, woodwinds, organic |
| Blue | E major or B♭ minor | Strings, smooth, cool |
| Indigo / violet | A minor or D♭ major | Ambient pad, ethereal |
| White | C major or modal Lydian | Bell, choir, pure tone |
| Black | C minor or F minor | Sub-bass, drone, dark pad |
| Pink | A major or G major | Flute, bell, light |
| Brown | F major | Acoustic guitar, warm pad |
| Grey | A minor | Felt piano, neutral pad |
| Gold | D major or E♭ major | Brass, bell, regal |

## Texture-to-Timbre Mappings

| Visual texture | Audio quality |
|---|---|
| Smooth | Pure sine waves, sustained, legato |
| Rough / gritty | Saw waves, distortion, granular |
| Sharp edges | Square waves, percussive attack |
| Soft / blurry | Filtered, reverb, pad |
| Dense / busy | Polyphonic, multiple voices |
| Sparse / minimal | Solo line, lots of silence |
| Geometric / mechanical | Quantised rhythm, electronic |
| Organic / natural | Acoustic, wooden, breath sounds |
