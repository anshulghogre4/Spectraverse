# Music Theory: Intervals and Consonance

## Consonance vs Dissonance Hierarchy

From most consonant (stable, restful) to most dissonant (tense, unresolved):

1. **Perfect unison** (P1) — same note, total stability
2. **Perfect octave** (P8) — most consonant interval
3. **Perfect fifth** (P5) — open, strong, "heroic" (Star Wars theme)
4. **Perfect fourth** (P4) — consonant in melodic context, dissonant when stacked
5. **Major third** (M3) — bright, cheerful, sweet
6. **Minor third** (m3) — sad, soft, melancholy
7. **Major sixth** (M6) — warm, romantic
8. **Minor sixth** (m6) — yearning, nostalgic
9. **Major second** (M2) — mild dissonance, neighbouring
10. **Minor seventh** (m7) — bluesy, jazzy
11. **Major seventh** (M7) — sharp dissonance, longing
12. **Minor second** (m2) — strong dissonance, semitone clash
13. **Tritone** (TT) — "the devil's interval", maximum dissonance — used in horror, tension

## Emotional Mapping per Interval

| Interval | Emotional character | Sound design use |
|---|---|---|
| Perfect 5th | Heroic, open, strong | Power chords, fanfares, victory |
| Major 3rd | Happy, bright | Major chord triads, pop |
| Minor 3rd | Sad, gentle | Minor triads, lullabies |
| Tritone | Evil, unsettling, tense | Horror film scores, dissonance |
| Minor 2nd | Sharp, fearful, urgent | Jaws theme, suspense |
| Octave | Pure, complete, doubling | Bass octaves for power |
| Diminished 5th | Dark, ominous (same as tritone) | Villain themes |
| Augmented 4th | Mystical, otherworldly (same as tritone) | Sci-fi, magic |

## Specific Style Mappings

### Horror sound design uses
- Tritone intervals (C → F♯)
- Minor 2nd clusters (close-spaced semitones)
- Reverse-pedal effects
- Detuned unisons (slightly out-of-tune octaves)
- Inverted minor chords

### Romantic / emotional music uses
- Suspended chords (sus4, sus2) — tension that resolves
- 6/9 chords — warm, lush
- Major 7th intervals — yearning, dreamy
- Pedal tones in bass

### Jazz and "cool" sound uses
- Minor 7th chords
- 9th and 11th extensions
- Dorian mode
- Tritone substitution in cadences

### Spiritual / sacred sound uses
- Drone notes (sustained low fundamentals)
- Open 5ths (no 3rd) — feels ancient, monastic
- Just intonation (pure ratios, no temperament)
- Pythagorean tuning for solo voice
