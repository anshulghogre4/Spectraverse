# Film Score Techniques: Mood-Based Sound Design

This knowledge base entry documents practical sound-design rules used by professional film composers, mapped to the 7 creative-mode styles in SpectraVerse.

## Horror

Composers: Bernard Herrmann (*Psycho*), Mica Levi (*Under the Skin*), Krzysztof Penderecki (*The Shining*).

Techniques:
- **Tritone interval** dominates (the diabolus in musica)
- **Sub-bass drones** below 60 Hz — felt rather than heard
- **Inverted pulse / accelerating rhythm** — heartbeat going wrong
- **Cluster chords** (minor 2nds stacked)
- **Reverse reverb** on sharp attacks — sounds "wrong"
- **Detuning** by 5-15 cents creates unease
- **Sudden silence** punctuates fortissimo stabs
- Avoid major thirds entirely

Recommended params: C minor or F minor; BPM 50-70 (slow heartbeat); organ + low cello + sub-bass; heavy reverb (0.85+); intensity 0.9; complexity 0.4 (sparse).

## Emotional / Romantic

Composers: John Williams, Howard Shore, Joe Hisaishi.

Techniques:
- **Minor 3rds and 6ths** in melody
- **Suspended 4ths resolving down** — the "sigh"
- **Piano + cello + string pad** — the classic trinity
- **Rubato** (flexible timing) — never strictly on the beat
- **Soft attack envelopes** — no sharp sounds
- **Slow swells with 3-second crescendos**

Recommended params: A minor or D minor; BPM 65-80; piano + cello + pad; reverb 0.7; intensity 0.5; complexity 0.5.

## Funny / Playful

Composers: Danny Elfman (*Beetlejuice*), Carl Stalling (Looney Tunes), Klaus Badelt.

Techniques:
- **Pizzicato strings** with comic timing
- **Glissando upward** — surprise
- **Whole-tone runs** — tipsy, falling
- **Mixed bright instruments**: vibraphone + piano + glockenspiel
- **Sudden register changes** — high to low for pratfalls
- **Augmented 4ths to perfect 5ths** — "wonky" resolutions

Recommended params: C major or D major; BPM 120-140; piano + vibraphone + guitar; reverb 0.2 (dry); intensity 0.7; complexity 0.7.

## Electrifying

Composers: Daft Punk, Hans Zimmer (*Tron Legacy*), Justice.

Techniques:
- **Sawtooth lead synth** — bright, harmonically rich
- **Sidechain compression** — pumping rhythm
- **Delay set to dotted 1/8** — drives forward motion
- **High-pass on the kick** — punchy not muddy
- **Major thirds in upper register**
- **8th-note arpeggios at 140+ BPM**

Recommended params: E major or G major; BPM 140-160; sawtooth lead + driving bass + claps; delay + distortion; reverb 0.4; intensity 0.95.

## Bassy

Composers: Skrillex, Massive Attack, dubstep producers generally.

Techniques:
- **Sub-bass fundamental at 40-80 Hz**
- **Heavy parallel compression** (NY-style)
- **Wobble bass** (LFO on filter cutoff)
- **Half-time drums** — beat lands "behind"
- **Minimum harmonic content** in the high range

Recommended params: F minor; BPM 70-95 (or 140 half-time); pad + organ in lowest octave; compression + distortion; intensity 1.0; complexity 0.3.

## Spiritual

Composers: Arvo Pärt, John Tavener, Hildegard von Bingen.

Techniques:
- **Drone tonic** sustained throughout
- **Perfect 5ths and octaves only** (organum)
- **Lydian mode** (raised 4th — F G A B C D E F)
- **Cathedral-scale reverb** (1.5-2.5 second decay)
- **Glissandi between notes** (smooth, no attacks)
- **Slow tempo**, no obvious beat

Recommended params: F Lydian or C Lydian; BPM 50-65; organ + pad + cello drone; reverb 0.95 (cathedral); intensity 0.5; complexity 0.3.

## Experimental

Composers: György Ligeti, Karlheinz Stockhausen, Aphex Twin.

Techniques:
- **Atonal / 12-tone** material
- **Granular synthesis** textures
- **Microtones** between standard pitches
- **Polyrhythms** (3 against 4 against 5)
- **Sound objects** instead of melodies
- **Generative / chance** processes

Recommended params: Atonal or chromatic; BPM 90-130 (irregular); pad + guitar + vibraphone clusters; distortion + delay; reverb 0.6; intensity 0.7; complexity 0.9.
